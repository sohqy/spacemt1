deep space 2018

MIXED TOUR 1

7 games | 3 wins | 4 losses.

Summary:

This write up looks into the details of not only the overall team performance over the weekend, individual player tendencies and strengths that may have been overlooked, as well as trying to answer the question of whether Deep Space truly plays as a mixed team. It was found that while the team manages to convert most of the high turnover points, the following point is almost always conceded. Deep Space's red zone statistics show that it makes full use of the squad, and has a good distribution of players scoring goals. However, it also shows a significant dearth of assists thrown by women. Some action points to consider can be found at the end of the document.

Are we playing as a mixed team?

This section seeks to answer that question. Just because a team may call Men/Women for an equal number of points, that does not mean that the team plays as a mixed team. This section looks at the various metrics for the points played in each gender ratio. The term "F/M Point" denotes the gender that has four players on the pitch for that point.

48 Female Points | 72 Male Points

31 Female Goals | 30 Male Goals

18 Female Assists | 43 Male Assists

Deep Space has a higher conversion rate when playing Female points, although it should be noted that the actual conversion numbers are approximately equal. However, there is a significant lack of female players throwing the assist. For Female (Male) points, the expected proportion of a female (male) player assisting is approximately 60%. Isolating the female points show that most of the assists thrown are still completed by male players, while the male points show approximately the expected outcomes with 77% male Assists. Perhaps male handlers are picking up the disc in the red zone more frequently, or the red zone shots thrown by women tend to be riskier, or less likely to complete in comparison to those thrown by the men.

In comparison, the goal scoring gender behaves approximately as numerically expected - with females scoring 58% of the time in female points, and males scoring 57% of the time in male points.

Individual Results

The intention of this section is to highlight possible strengths or weaknesses within players, and not as a reflection of personal performance over the weekend. Whether the point started on O or D should be taken into account.

Average Points Played: 34.9

Average Conversion Rate on Offense: 54.5%

Average Conversion Rate on Defense: 49.2%

In looking at these individual numbers, all players that had been on the pitch for each point takes responsibility for the events in that point. These events are things like conversions, concessions, and turnovers.

For example, the pair of players that scores the goal and assist are awarded within those metrics. The remaining players on the pitch are awarded a conversion. This is assuming that: the player was doing their part in facilitating goal scoring. This could include, and is not limited to:

  • Running the systems and doing their jobs
  • Making space for their teammates
  • Using effective communication so that their teammates can work off of them.

Similarly, all players on the pitch are responsible for all the turnovers experienced within that point. This is because:

  • All drops and throwaways are results of high pressure situations
  • Pressured situations arise when players do not do their jobs correctly
  • Athletic D's from the other team is out of anyone's control, and no individual should hold sole responsibility for it

Number of Possessions

The violin chart is an estimate of the probability density using the given data. This is used here as a visual representation of the typical number of possessions for each outcome from each player. This maps out player correlations with point turnovers.

For each player, the points they have played have been sorted according to whether that point was conceded (blue) or converted (green). Each violin plot shows the median and mean for the player, and outlier points are shown as dots. Box-zoom or select individual names from the legend on the right to have a closer look at the distribution for each player. Since every point is taken into account for plotting the distribution, the narrow view for each individual can provide a rather warped perspective. The violins are also scaled based on the counts, thus providing a real feel of the number of points each player has played.

The point with the highest number of possessions (7) is an outlier for all players on that point. However, the remaining high possession points are not anomalies for all your players. Perhaps these players have a tendency to contribute to a higher turnover rate.

In general, a high number of possessions (or turnovers) within a point signals:

  • Good defense but inefficient offense
  • Poor offense on both teams
  • Good defence on both teams.

The common theme through the signals is an inefficient or poor offense - which could be further attributed to a large number of reasons such as:

  • Lack of communication, or chemistry between specific players
  • Not running the systems correctly
  • Poor decision making or forcing options that are not absolutely on.

Most of the players have a relatively high spread of points in terms of the number of possessions - while this eliminates the likelihood of issues between specific players, and highlights a possible issue with the systems.

Whilst it is difficult to pinpoint the exact reasons for the high turnovers without looking at how the turnover happens (i.e. whether it is an offensive or defensive effort), there are some scenarios with clear signals:

In the most efficient, ideal offense, the team would take one possession to score. This means that the distribution would look like a spike centered at 1. Efficient scoring can be seen if the player has a mean of 1-1.5 possessions in their converted points. The only way to score below 1 for converted points would be through a callahan. Player means that deviate higher than 1 signals the possibility of the player contributing to inefficient offense.

The ideal distribution for conceded points of course would not exist, but a low mean between 0-1 would mean that the other team walks the disc into their endzone easily. No Deep Space player has a conceded mean below 1, but there are players who have significantly higher number of zero possession concessions than others. Since the team has yet to be split into O and D lines for Mixed Tour 1, this should be an area of consideration for the strengths and weaknesses of these players.

Action Points

Since the team offense can be considered to be the main culprit for unideal performance, there needs to be a closer look at the offensive systems, and how the players work together.

It would be useful to identify the cause for the disparity in red zone stats - we've previously identified it to possibly be because women are less confident in taking the shots, or are taking riskier shots.

Considering mental toughness as a team, and working on maintaining high performance throughout an entire game.
